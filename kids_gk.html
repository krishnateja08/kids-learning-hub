The Amazing Human Body
You are a walking miracle!
🧠

Brain

Control centre of your body. Has 86 billion nerve cells! Uses 20% of all your body's energy. Weighs about 1.4 kg.

❤️

Heart

Beats 100,000 times a day! Pumps 7,000 litres of blood daily. Size of your fist. Made of special muscle.

🫁

Lungs

We breathe 20,000 times a day! Right lung is bigger than left. They float on water — only organ that does!

🦷

Teeth

Children have 20 milk teeth. Adults have 32 teeth. Enamel is the hardest substance in the human body!

👁️

Eyes

Can see 10 million colours! Each eye has 130 million light receptor cells. Blink 10,000 times per day!

🦴

Bones

206 bones in an adult. Babies are born with 270 bones — they fuse as you grow! The femur is the longest bone.

🩸

Blood

5 litres of blood in your body. Red blood cells live 120 days. Blood has 4 types: A, B, AB, O.

👂

Ears

Help you hear AND balance! The smallest bones in the body (hammer, anvil, stirrup) are inside your ear!

🧬

DNA – Your Body's Blueprint!

Every cell in your body contains DNA — a set of instructions that makes you unique! If you stretched all the DNA from one human cell, it would be 2 metres long. All the DNA in your body would reach the Sun and back 600 times!

📊 Fun Maths Tricks
✖️

Multiply by 9 Trick

9×1=9, 9×2=18, ... digits always add to 9! (18→1+8=9, 27→2+7=9) Try it!

💫

The 11 Trick

11×12: put the digits 1 and 2 on sides, sum in middle → 1,3,2 = 132! (works up to 11×18)

🔁

Any Number × 0

Any number multiplied by ZERO is always ZERO! 1000 × 0 = 0!

🌟

Powers of 2

2,4,8,16,32,64... Double each time! Computers use this — everything is 0s and 1s!

Weather & Seasons
Why does weather happen? Let's find out!
🍂 Four Seasons
☀️
Summer
Hot and sunny! Earth tilts toward the Sun. Days are longer. In India: March–June
🌧️
Monsoon/Autumn
Rain, wind, and relief! In India: June–September. Life-giving rains for farms!
❄️
Winter
Cool and cold! Earth tilts away from Sun. Days are shorter. In India: Nov–Feb
🌸
Spring
Flowers bloom! Perfect temperatures. Earth returns to upright position. In India: Feb–March
🌈 Weather Wonders
🌈

Rainbow

Sunlight bends inside raindrops and splits into 7 colours: VIBGYOR! You need both Sun and rain at the same time.

⚡

Lightning

Electricity built up in storm clouds! When it gets too much, ZAP — it jumps to Earth. Thunder is the SOUND of lightning.

🌪️

Tornado

A spinning column of air touching the ground. Can spin at 400+ km/h! Strongest tornadoes can lift cars and houses!

🌀

Cyclone

A giant spinning storm over the ocean. India's east coast (including AP!) faces cyclones in Oct–Dec season.

☁️

Clouds

Made of millions of tiny water droplets floating in air! A fluffy cumulus cloud weighs about 500 tonnes — yet floats!

❄️

Snowflake

Every single snowflake is unique! Each one has 6 sides but no two are ever exactly the same shape. Amazing!

🌡️ Climate Change — Explained Simply
🏭

Greenhouse Effect

CO₂ from factories and cars traps the Sun's heat like a blanket around Earth. Too much heat = climate change!

🌊

Rising Seas

Ice caps melt → sea level rises → coastal cities flood! Mumbai and Visakhapatnam are at risk by 2100.

🌲

Deforestation

Cutting trees removes CO₂ absorbers. 1 tree absorbs ~22 kg of CO₂ per year. PLANT MORE TREES!

Mythology Stories
Epic tales from ancient India, Greece & Egypt!
🇮🇳 Indian Mythology
🇮🇳 Indian
🐘 How Ganesha Got His Elephant Head
Goddess Parvati created Ganesha from sandalwood paste and asked him to guard her door. Lord Shiva, not recognizing him, fought with Ganesha and — in the heat of battle — accidentally cut off his head! Horrified, Shiva promised to replace it with the first head he found. The first creature found was an elephant, and so Ganesha received his magnificent elephant head! Now he is worshipped as the Remover of Obstacles, and we pray to Ganesha first before starting anything new.
💡 Moral: Even from accidents, great things can emerge!
🇮🇳 Indian
🏹 The Mahabharata's Lesson on Focus
Guru Dronacharya taught the Pandava and Kaurava princes archery. One day he hung a wooden bird from a tree and asked each student: "What do you see?" Everyone answered "The tree, the branches, the leaves, the bird…" When Arjuna's turn came, he said "I see only the eye of the bird." Drona smiled and said "SHOOT!" — Arjuna hit the target perfectly. Only he saw what mattered.
💡 Moral: Focus completely on your goal — ignore all distractions!
🇮🇳 Indian
🌊 Samudra Manthan — Churning of the Ocean
The Devas (gods) and Asuras (demons) together churned the great cosmic ocean to find Amrita — the nectar of immortality. They used Mount Mandara as a churning rod and the serpent Vasuki as a rope. From this churning emerged many treasures: Lakshmi (goddess of wealth), Kamadhenu (wish-granting cow), and finally Dhanvantari carrying the pot of Amrita!
💡 Moral: Great rewards come from great teamwork and effort!
🏛️ Greek Mythology
🏛️ Greek
⚡ Zeus — King of the Gods
Zeus was the king of all gods on Mount Olympus. He controlled lightning bolts and ruled the sky. His brothers Poseidon ruled the seas and Hades ruled the underworld. Together they overthrew the Titans (giant beings) who had ruled before them. Zeus is also famous for disguising himself and visiting humans on Earth!
💡 Famous symbol: The thunderbolt — also found on India's Ashoka Chakra!
🏛️ Greek
🌞 Icarus and the Wax Wings
Daedalus, a great inventor, was trapped on an island. He built wings from feathers and wax for himself and his son Icarus to escape. He warned Icarus: "Don't fly too high near the Sun, and don't fly too low near the sea." But Icarus was thrilled by flying — he soared higher and higher. The Sun melted his wings and he fell into the sea.
💡 Moral: Listen to warnings. Too much pride leads to downfall.
🌅 Egyptian Mythology
🌅 Egyptian
☀️ Ra — The Sun God
Ra was the most powerful Egyptian god. Every morning he rode his golden boat across the sky (that's the Sun moving!). At night he sailed through the underworld, fighting the serpent Apophis, and emerged victorious each morning. This is why Egyptians believed sunrise was Ra's daily victory over darkness. Pharaohs called themselves "sons of Ra"!
💡 Fun fact: Ra is one of the reasons Egypt built the pyramids to honor the Sun!
